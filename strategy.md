# Shop strategy hypothesis (Version 4)

## Hypothesis

I hypothesize that the biggest issue with my long run profitability is not early game execution on pure 
potions, but a mid game ceiling on mixed potions. As of now I do a good job of setting targets to figure 
out how much ml I buy for bottling plus a reserve favoring cheaper barrels. Then trying to prioritize 
making mixed potions with whatever I have over making pure potions. Once I have enough gold and raw ml, my 
bottling and barrel logic pushes higher volumes of red, green, and blue mixed potions like purple, yellow, 
and teal. I think my most important change that I need to make is once I hit this mid level game, I need 
to prioritize buying dark barrels and making potions with it. Right now, dark potions stay under produced 
because they depend on dark ml that I do not prioritize as aggressively as RGB. At the same time, my 
catalog only lists potions with positive ledger stock, and the assignment caps visible offers at 6 I 
belive so if I never reliably stock additional mixed SKUs, I effectively cap my quality for customer 
classes that prefer complex or dark heavy blends.

What changed after collecting metrics is how I justify that hypothesis: the charts and barrel snapshots give concrete patterns (even on a recently burned shop with thin history) rather than intuition alone.

## What the metrics showed

**Metric 1 (sales by hour).** Red shows up across more hours than other potions, green sells repeatedly but less steadily, blue and yellow appear in focused bursts. That means red is a baseline stock item and I should be treating yellow and blue as more timing sensitive until volume grows.

**Metric 2 (barrel offerings).** Logging wholesale menus confirmed that offer sets and cost per ml change by snapshot. Medium class barrels dominated some windows while large barrels appeared later and were the cheapest per ml when offered. So timing purchases and not always grabbing the first affordable barrel can matter for my shop and maximizing profit.

**Metric 3 (demographics).** Demand is not random. Class and species both cluster on certain colors (e.g. warriors on red, seers on green, runesmiths on blue, hunters / wolf warriors on yellow in my small bit of data). That implies targeted stocking and promotions are better and beat a one size catalog.

**Metric 4 (gold revenue).** On my post burn window, red dominated total gold, ahead of green and yellow, with blue lowest. That reinforces red as a revenue anchor for now, konwing that mixed SKUs would likely climb once I run several more days without burning and keep cyan/purple/dark bottles reliably on hand.

## Changes I believe matter most

1. Prioritize dark barrels and dark using recipes once mid game ml is stable so Rainbow and dark bottled SKUs actually appear in the catalog, not only RGB pairwise mixes.

2. Keep red reliably deep (baseline demand + revenue in my current data), while watch yellow and blue as bursts of SKUs until history lengthens.

3. Use barrel snapshots over time to buy during cheaper per ml windows when gold and capacity allow, instead of automatically clearing ml shortages with mediocre wholesale value.

4. Tune pricing with reputation in mind. I lowered yellow toward 45 gold because value stars were low even when other ratings on yellow looked fine. 

## Why this matters for profit

Profit is not “sell everything you brew once.” Reputation drives repeat visits; it explicitly rewards value, quality, reliability, and recognition by class. If I only rotate RGB mixes and pure basics, I leave quality and recognition on the table for buyers who want darker or more complex blends. Metric 4 also reminds me that gold revenue can diverge from units sold because I need high gold making anchors like red while expanding mixes that will matter after the shop has more uninterrupted runtime and my shop grows.
