# Shop strategy hypothesis (Version 4)

## Hypothesis

I hypothesize that the biggest issue with my long run profitability is not early game execution on pure potions, but a mid game ceiling on mixed potions. As of now I do a good job of setting targets to figure out how much ml I buy for bottling plus a reserve favoring cheaper barrels. Then trying to prioritize making mixed potions with whatever I have over making pure potions. Once I have enough gold and raw ml, my bottling and barrel logic pushes higher volumes of red, green, and blue mixed potions like purple, yellow, and teal. I think my most important change that I need to make is once I hit this mid level game, I need to prioritize buying dark barrels and making potions with it. Right now, dark potions stays under produced because they depend on dark ml that I do not prioritize as aggressively as RGB. At the same time, my catalog only lists potions with positive ledger stock, and the assignment caps visible offers at 6 I belive so if I never reliably stock additional mixed SKUs, I effectively cap my quality for customer classes that prefer complex or dark heavy blends.

## Why this matters for profit

This matters for my profit because profit is not only sell everything I make. Reputation brings back customers and my reputation with only my 3 stocked, non pure potions kinda makes my quality struggle because customers may want other potions no matter how efficiently I brew those three potions.
